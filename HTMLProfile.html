The Life of Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur and co-founder of Apple Inc., which is recognized as an extremely revolutionary company and a pioneer of personal computing. Jobs is seen as a visionary in music, animation, and mobile computing.

Early Life

Steve Jobs, Steve Jobs was born on February 24th, 1955, in San Francisco California. He grew up in Cupertino, and became heavily interested in electronics from an early age. Jobs attended Reed College for a short period before he dropped out to pursue new technical projects.

Career and Innovations

Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne founded Apple in 1976. Jobs helped create products such as the Apple II, and Macintosh. Later in his career, he created the iPod, iPhone and iPad. Jobs left Apple in 1985, founding NeXT and purchasing Pixar. When Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, he transformed it into one of the most valuable companies in the world.

Biggest Achievements

  1. Co-founded Apple Inc..
  2. Created the Macintosh computer, which was the first mass-market GUI-based computer.
  3. Revolutionized many industries with the release of the iPhone.

Impact on Technology

  • Set new design standards with his products
  • nspired a generation of entrepreneurs through his emphasis on innovation.
  • Completely changed how people interact with technology. He made electronic tools a part of everyday life.
